# Daily Threat Intel Digest - 2026-04-25

## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[NEW] CISA and NCSC warn of "Firestarter" backdoor surviving Cisco firewall updates**
State-sponsored threat actor UAT-4356 is deploying a custom backdoor dubbed "Firestarter" on Cisco Firepower and Secure Firewall devices running ASA or FTD software. The implant achieves persistent access that actively survives firmware updates, security patches, and graceful reboots by hooking into the LINA core process and modifying the `CSP_MOUNT_LIST` boot configuration file. Attackers are gaining initial access by exploiting two n-day vulnerabilities—CVE-2025-20333 (a missing authorization issue) and CVE-2025-20362 (a buffer overflow) [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/firestarter-malware-survives-cisco-firewall-updates-security-patches/); [CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/hackers-exploit-cisco-firepower-devices-using-n-day-vulnerabilities-to-gain-unauthorized-access/)]. 

Following initial exploitation, the attackers deploy the Line Viper shellcode loader to establish VPN sessions and steal administrative credentials, certificates, and private keys. The Firestarter backdoor is then deployed to maintain long-term access, activating via a specially crafted WebVPN request containing hardcoded "magic bytes" to execute attacker-supplied payloads directly in memory. CISA observed this chain successfully compromising a federal civilian executive branch agency in early September 2025, with the implant remaining active before the agency applied patches. Cisco strongly recommends re-imaging and upgrading affected devices to fixed releases immediately. Defenders can check for compromise by running the command `show kernel process | include lina_cs`—any output indicates a compromised device. Because the persistence mechanism is tied to a graceful reboot, a physical power cycle (cold restart) will temporarily remove the malware, though this carries a risk of database or disk corruption.

**[NEW] ADT confirms data breach following ShinyHunters extortion threat**
Home security giant ADT has confirmed unauthorized access to customer data following a ShinyHunters listing on the group's dark web leak site threatening to publish 10 million stolen records [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/adt-confirms-data-breach-after-shinyhunters-leak-threat/)]. The breach was detected and terminated on April 20. ADT stated the stolen data was limited to names, phone numbers, and addresses. In a small percentage of cases, dates of birth and the last four digits of Social Security numbers or Tax IDs were also exposed. Critically, no payment information was accessed, and customer home security systems were not compromised. ShinyHunters claimed they breached the company via a voice phishing (vishing) attack that compromised an employee’s Okta single sign-on (SSO) account, subsequently using it to access and exfiltrate data from ADT's Salesforce instance.

**[UPDATE] Over 10,000 Zimbra servers vulnerable to actively exploited XSS flaw**
Following CISA’s addition of CVE-2025-48700 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog earlier this week, internet watchdog Shadowserver reports that over 10,500 Zimbra Collaboration Suite instances—primarily located in Asia and Europe—remain unpatched and exposed to ongoing active exploitation [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-says-zimbra-flaw-now-exploited-over-10k-servers-vulnerable/)]. The critical XSS flaw requires no user interaction to trigger; an attacker achieves arbitrary JavaScript execution simply by having a target view a maliciously crafted email in the Zimbra Classic UI. CISA has ordered federal civilian executive branch agencies to patch their systems immediately. The vulnerability has become a favored vector for state-backed groups, with APT28 (Fancy Bear) recently exploiting a similar Zimbra XSS flaw in phishing campaigns targeting Ukrainian government entities.

## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[NEW] BlackFile extortion group targets retail and hospitality via vishing campaigns**
A new financially motivated threat group tracked as BlackFile (also known as CL-CRI-1116, UNC6671, and Cordial Spider) has been conducting widespread data theft and extortion attacks against retail and hospitality organizations since February 2026. Unit 42 has linked the group with moderate confidence to "The Com," a loosely organized network of English-speaking cybercriminals [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-blackfile-extortion-gang-targets-retail-and-hospitality-orgs/)]. 

BlackFile operators initiate attacks by calling employees from spoofed VoIP numbers and impersonating corporate IT helpdesk staff. The actors direct victims to fake login pages that capture credentials and one-time passcodes. Using these stolen credentials, the attackers register their own devices to bypass multi-factor authentication (MFA), subsequently escalating their access to executive-level accounts by scraping internal employee directories. The group then abuses standard API functions to steal massive volumes of data from Salesforce and SharePoint servers, specifically searching for files containing terms like "confidential" and "SSN." Exfiltrated data is published to the group's dark web leak site before seven-figure ransom demands are issued via compromised employee email accounts. In some cases, the group has escalated pressure by swatting senior executives of compromised companies.

**[UPDATE] Bitwarden CLI supply chain attack shows worm-like self-propagation**
Following yesterday's initial reports of a malicious Bitwarden CLI package, Unit 42 has released deeper analysis revealing that the threat actor TeamPCP designed the malware with worm-like capabilities that automatically backdoor any npm package the victim has permission to publish [[Palo Alto Networks](https://www.paloaltonetworks.com/blog/cloud-security/bitwardencli-supply-chain-attack/); [Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/monitoring-npm-supply-chain-attacks/)]. The scoped package `@bitwarden/cli@2026.4.0` was published on April 22 and downloaded thousands of times before being flagged. The payload autonomously harvests SSH keys, `.npmrc` tokens, `.env` files, cloud provider credentials (AWS, Azure, GCP), GitHub Actions secrets, and AI/MCP configurations. Organizations are urged to immediately search all `package.json` and lockfiles for `@bitwarden/cli` version 2026.4.0, and if found, assume all credentials on the affected machine and connected CI/CD environments are compromised and require rotation.

## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] Pack2TheRoot flaw allows unprivileged Linux users to gain root access**
A vulnerability dubbed "Pack2TheRoot" (CVE-2026-41651, CVSS 8.8) in the PackageKit daemon allows local, unprivileged Linux users to install or remove system packages, enabling full privilege escalation to root. Deutsche Telekom's Red Team discovered that the `pkcon install` command could execute without authentication under certain conditions, and they later used the Claude Opus AI tool to help map the full exploit chain [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-pack2theroot-flaw-gives-hackers-root-linux-access/)]. 

The flaw has persisted in the PackageKit daemon for almost 12 years, affecting all versions from 1.0.2 (released November 2014) through 1.3.4. Researchers confirmed the vulnerability is exploitable on default installations of Ubuntu Desktop and Server, Debian Desktop, RockyLinux Desktop, and Fedora Desktop and Server. Because PackageKit is pre-installed and enabled out-of-the-box on many major Linux distributions, the true scope of vulnerable systems is vast. The flaw was patched in version 1.3.5, released earlier this week alongside minimal technical details to allow patches to propagate. Defenders can check for exposure by running `dpkg -l | grep -i packagekit` or `rpm -qa | grep -i packagekit` and verifying the daemon status with `systemctl status packagekit`. Exploitation attempts can be detected by monitoring system logs for PackageKit daemon crash events resulting from assertion failures.

**[NEW] Microsoft Entra ID flaw allowed Agent ID Administrator role to hijack enterprise service principals**
A now-patched vulnerability in Microsoft Entra ID’s newly introduced Agent Identity Platform allowed users assigned the "Agent ID Administrator" role to take full control of arbitrary service principals across an organization's tenant. Researchers at SilverFort demonstrated that attackers could exploit a scoping gap in the shared identity architecture to assign themselves as owners of any service principal, generate new credentials for it, and fully authenticate as the target identity—potentially gaining instant access to CI/CD pipelines, automation workflows, and security tools [[CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/hackers-can-abuse-agent-id-administrator-role-to-hijack-service-principals/)]. Microsoft rolled out a fix by April 9, 2026, ensuring the role can no longer modify ownership of non-agent service principals. Organizations should immediately audit changes to service principal ownership, track newly created credentials for sensitive identities, and monitor privileged role assignments for misuse.

## 📋 Policy & Industry News

**Section 702 FISA reauthorization stalls as April 30 deadline looms**
The latest legislative attempt to extend spying powers under Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act has failed to placate civil liberties critics. House Speaker Mike Johnson introduced a bill to extend the warrantless surveillance powers for three years, accompanied by a provision stating government officials cannot use Section 702 to intentionally target Americans. However, digital rights groups uniformly dismissed the language as "smoke and mirrors," noting that the core issue is the incidental collection and subsequent searching of U.S. person communications without a warrant, not the intentional targeting of Americans. With the April 30 deadline approaching and a 10-day stopgap reauthorization set to expire, the program faces an imminent expiration if a compromise is not reached [[CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/section-702-fisa-reauthorization-bill-leaves-critics-unimpressed/)].

**[NEW] OpenAI launches GPT-5.5 Bio Bug Bounty to proactively mitigate biological threats**
OpenAI has launched a specialized bug bounty program inviting vetted bio red teamers and cybersecurity researchers to find "universal jailbreaks" capable of forcing GPT-5.5 to bypass its safety filters and answer a strict five-question biosafety challenge. The initiative aims to identify and patch safeguards before malicious actors can weaponize advanced AI capabilities to accelerate harmful biological research. The top prize for a successful universal jailbreak is $25,000, with testing taking place within a controlled, monitored environment from April 28 to July 27, 2026. Approved participants are required to sign strict NDAs preventing public disclosure of prompts or findings [[CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/gpt-5-5-bio-bug-bounty-launched/)].

**Microsoft and Anthropic push forward on identity and browser security architectures**
Microsoft has announced the upcoming rollout of Microsoft Entra passkeys on Windows devices, enabling phishing-resistant, FIDO2-based passwordless authentication for users on unmanaged, personal, and shared devices via Windows Hello (face, fingerprint, or PIN). The feature, expected to reach general availability by mid-June 2026, cryptographically binds passkeys to local devices, preventing remote theft during phishing or malware attacks [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-to-roll-out-entra-passkeys-on-windows-in-late-april/)]. Separately, privacy researchers have raised concerns over Anthropic's Claude Desktop application for macOS, which reportedly silently installs a Native Messaging bridge across multiple Chromium-based browsers—enabling out-of-sandbox browser automation capabilities—without explicit user consent. Organizations using Claude Desktop on macOS are advised to audit systems for the `com.anthropic.claude_browser_extension.json` configuration file [[CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/claude-desktop-reportedly-adds-browser-access-bridge-for-multiple-chromium-based-browsers/)].
