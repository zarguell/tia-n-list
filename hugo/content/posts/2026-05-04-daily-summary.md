**[UPDATE] cPanel CVE-2026-41940 — CISA adds to KEV with 4-day patch deadline as 40,000+ servers confirmed compromised in government and military targeting**

CISA has added CVE-2026-41940 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, requiring federal agencies to patch within four days. The escalation comes as SecurityWeek reports over 40,000 servers likely compromised worldwide, with Shadowserver confirming 44,000 unique IPs scanning honeypots — numbers that spiked sharply after watchTowr Labs' public disclosure on April 28 but began dropping as of May 3 as organizations apply patches. New details from GBHackers reveal that threat actors weaponized the public PoC within days of disclosure to target government and military organizations across Southeast Asia, including the Philippine Coast Guard and the Lao Ministry of National Defense. The attackers combined cPanel exploitation with a separate custom exploit chain against an Indonesian defense-sector training portal, ultimately exfiltrating over 4 GB of documents including Chinese railway infrastructure data containing national identification numbers, bank account details, and phone numbers. After gaining access, the attackers deployed OpenVPN and Ligolo tunneling tools disguised as Linux services for persistent network pivoting. **[UPDATE] Instructure breach confirmed — ShinyHunters claims 275 million records and 3.65 TB stolen across ~9,000 schools**

Instructure has confirmed that the Canvas LMS breach exposed user names, email addresses, student ID numbers, and private messages between users. The company states it has found no evidence that passwords, dates of birth, government identifiers, or financial information were compromised. ShinyHunters, the extortion group behind the September 2025 Instructure breach, has claimed responsibility for the new incident, alleging 3.65 TB of data stolen spanning 275 million individuals across nearly 9,000 institutions worldwide, including access to Instructure's Salesforce instance. Instructure has deployed security patches, rotated application keys (customers must re-authorize API access), revoked privileged credentials, and restored Canvas Data 2 and Beta services. The company has not confirmed the attacker's scale claims or whether extortion is being attempted. **[UPDATE] DigiCert root certificates falsely flagged as malware by Microsoft Defender — caused by overly broad response to real certificate compromise**

Microsoft Defender caused global disruption on May 3 by incorrectly flagging two legitimate DigiCert root certificate hashes (0563B8630D62D75ABBC8AB1E4BDFB5A899B24D43 and DDFB16CD4931C973A2037D3FC83A4D775D05E4) as Trojan:Win32/Cerdigent.A!dha, removing them from the Windows trust store and breaking TLS validation. Some administrators performed unnecessary OS reinstalls believing their systems were compromised. The false positive was an overly broad detection response to a real DigiCert security incident: in early April, Chinese crime group GoldenEyeDog (APT-Q-27) phished DigiCert support staff to obtain initialization codes for EV code-signing certificates, which were then used to sign Zhong Stealer malware distributed under legitimate company names including Lenovo and Kingston. DigiCert revoked 60 certificates, but Microsoft's Defender signature update overreached from targeting the compromised code-signing certificates to flagging unrelated DigiCert root certificates. Fixed in Security Intelligence update 1.449.430.0. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-defender-wrongly-flags-digicert-certs-as-trojan-win32-cerdigentadha/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/digicert-root-certificates-incorrectly-detected-as-malware/)]


## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[NEW] Microsoft Teams phishing campaign achieves 72% success rate through email bombing and fake IT support impersonation**

Since early 2026, threat actors have been combining aggressive email bombing — flooding a victim's inbox with thousands of spam messages to create urgency — with direct Microsoft Teams messages impersonating the company's IT helpdesk. eSentire reports the two-step attack achieves a staggering 72% success rate. After gaining remote access through Windows Quick Assist, attackers use the legitimate WinSCP file transfer tool to exfiltrate data, or deploy a Java-based payload from a ZIP archive named "Email-Deployment-Process-System.zip." The campaigns use bulletproof hosting providers (NKtelecom INC, WorkTitans B.V., Global Connectivity Solutions LLP, GWY IT PTY LTD) and create convincing personas with freshly registered .onmicrosoft.com accounts labeled "Windows Security Help Desk." Organizations should restrict external Teams communications, configure prominent warning banners for external users, and train employees to verify IT support requests through established internal channels. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/hackers-weaponize-teams-chats/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/microsoft-teams-phishing-surge/)]

**[NEW] FEMITBOT platform abuses Telegram Mini Apps for crypto scams and Android malware at scale**

CTM360 researchers have uncovered FEMITBOT, a fraud platform that weaponizes Telegram's Mini App feature to run phishing pages inside Telegram's built-in WebView browser. Victims interact with bots that launch Mini Apps displaying fake cryptocurrency dashboards with countdown timers and limited-time offers. When attempting to withdraw "earnings," victims are prompted to deposit funds or complete referral tasks (classic advance-fee fraud). The platform's shared backend (identified by API responses containing "Welcome to join the FEMITBOT platform") is designed for reuse across campaigns with swappable branding, languages, and themes. Major brands impersonated include Apple, Coca-Cola, Disney, eBay, NVIDIA, and IBM. Some Mini Apps also distribute Android malware APKs impersonating BBC, NVIDIA, and CineTV. The infrastructure uses Meta and TikTok tracking pixels to measure and optimize scam conversion rates. ## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] FreeBSD DHCP client RCE (CVE-2026-42511) grants root access via rogue DHCP server on local network**

A critical vulnerability in FreeBSD's default IPv4 DHCP client, dhclient(8), allows any attacker on the same broadcast domain to execute arbitrary code as root by operating a rogue DHCP server. The flaw stems from improper sanitization of embedded double quotes in the BOOTP file field, which gets written into the local DHCP lease file and executed with root privileges when the lease is reprocessed during reboot or network service restart. Discovered by Joshua Rogers of AISLE Research Team, the vulnerability affects all supported FreeBSD branches (15.0, 14.x, 13.5). The FreeBSD Project has released patches; administrators should update immediately via `pkg upgrade -r FreeBSD-base` (15.0) or `freebsd-update fetch && freebsd-update install` (other versions). Organizations should also implement DHCP snooping on managed switches to block rogue DHCP responses. Systems not using dhclient(8) are unaffected. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/freebsd-dhcp-client-flaw-allows-remote-code-execution-as-root/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/freebsd-systems-at-risk-from-dhcp-client-rce-vulnerability/)]

**[NEW] MOVEit Automation authentication bypass (CVE-2026-4670) and privilege escalation (CVE-2026-5174) — patch immediately**

Progress Software has disclosed two critical vulnerabilities in MOVEit Automation discovered by Airbus SecLab researchers. CVE-2026-4670 is an authentication bypass and CVE-2026-5174 is a privilege escalation via improper input validation, both within the service backend command port interfaces. Successful exploitation grants administrative control. Patches are available for versions 2025.1.5, 2025.0.9, and 2024.1.8. Given MOVEit's history as a mass exploitation target (the 2023 Clop campaign affected thousands of organizations), organizations should prioritize patching immediately. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/moveit-authentication-bypass-vulnerability/)]

**[UPDATE] SAP npm supply chain attack (Mini Shai-Hulud) — new details on self-replicating worm using GitHub as dead drop**

New reporting from Endorlabs provides additional technical detail on the April 29 compromise of four SAP npm packages (mbt, @cap-js/sqlite, @cap-js/postgres, @cap-js/db-service). The attackers used two independent methods: a stolen long-lived npm automation token for the MBT package, and a compromised developer GitHub account combined with a misconfigured OIDC trust policy for the @cap-js packages. The malware downloads the Bun runtime to bypass Node.js-based security monitoring, deploys an 11.6 MB obfuscated payload using custom encryption (ctf-scramble-v2), and operates five parallel credential collectors targeting npm tokens, GitHub tokens, AWS/GCP/Azure credentials, Kubernetes service account tokens, SSH keys, and AI coding assistant tokens (Claude, Cursor). Stolen data is exfiltrated by creating public GitHub repositories under the victim's account with the description "A Mini Shai-Hulud has Appeared." The malware self-replicates by patching victims' own projects with the same malicious script when it detects publish-capable npm tokens, and persists by modifying VS Code configuration files. Organizations must rotate all credentials, check lockfiles for affected package versions, monitor CI/CD pipelines for unexpected changes, and migrate from static npm tokens to scoped OIDC publishing. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/malicious-sap-packages-steal-tokens/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/attackers-hijack-sap-npm/)]


## 🛡️ Defense & Detection

**[NEW] Qilin ransomware uses PowerShell Event ID 1149 query for stealthy RDP reconnaissance — detect via ScriptBlock logging**

Qilin ransomware operators have adopted a living-off-the-land reconnaissance technique that queries Windows Terminal Services Remote Connection Manager operational logs (Event ID 1149) to extract historical RDP authentication data — usernames, domains, and source client IPs — without triggering traditional network scan detection. The PowerShell query is delivered via a rogue ScreenConnect installation during initial compromise. Because there is practically no benign reason for a non-administrative process to query RDP operational logs for this event ID, security teams should treat any detection as a critical incident. PowerShell ScriptBlock Logging effectively captures these queries and should be enabled across all servers and endpoints. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/qilin-expands-breach-recon/)]

**[NEW] Trellix discloses source code repository breach — no evidence of product compromise or data distribution pipeline tampering**

Cybersecurity firm Trellix (endpoint security and XDR) has disclosed unauthorized access to a portion of its internal source code repository. The company states there is no evidence that production environments, the release pipeline, or customer-facing products were compromised, and no source code has been found actively exploited. External forensic experts and law enforcement are engaged. While the breach appears contained, stolen source code from a security vendor carries long-term risk if attackers analyze it to discover product vulnerabilities for future exploitation. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/hackers-breach-trellix-source-code-repository-in-unauthorized-access-incident/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/trellix-source-code-breach-exposes-repository/)]
