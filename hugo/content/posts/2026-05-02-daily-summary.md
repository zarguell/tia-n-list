## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[UPDATE] cPanelSniper PoC released as Shadowserver confirms 44,000 compromised IPs actively scanning**

Mass exploitation of CVE-2026-41940 has escalated dramatically since yesterday's report of zero-day exploitation dating back to February. Security researcher Yunus Emre Öztaş has published "cPanelSniper," a weaponized proof-of-concept framework that requires no external dependencies and runs entirely with standard Python libraries, automating a four-stage attack that grants immediate interactive root shell access to affected servers. The Shadowserver Foundation has confirmed at least 44,000 unique IP addresses are already compromised and actively scanning honeypots as of April 30. The exploit works by injecting CRLF characters into the HTTP Authorization header, writing malicious session data declaring `user=root` directly into disk-based session files before sanitization occurs. Passwords and MFA offer zero protection against this authentication bypass. cPanel/WHM powers tens of millions of domains globally, and the emergency patch must be manually installed via `/usr/local/cpanel/scripts/upcp` — automatic update channels do not deliver it. Organizations must immediately verify patching, audit session directories for indicators of compromise, rotate all administrative credentials, and monitor for unauthorized accounts. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/poc-exploit-for-cpanelsniper/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/cpanelsniper-poc-exploit-disclosed/)]

**[UPDATE] Copy Fail (CVE-2026-31431) added to CISA KEV as Microsoft warns of imminent broader exploitation**

The Copy Fail Linux kernel privilege escalation vulnerability has been added to CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, requiring federal agencies to remediate under BOD 22-01. Microsoft Defender's research team has published a detailed technical analysis confirming the vulnerability affects virtually every Linux distribution running kernels from 2017 onward, including Ubuntu 24.04 LTS, Amazon Linux 2023, RHEL 10.1, and SUSE 16. While exploitation has so far been observed primarily in proof-of-concept testing, Microsoft reports seeing preliminary activity consistent with threat actors preparing to weaponize the flaw at scale, with broader exploitation expected within days. The 732-byte Python exploit requires only standard library modules and no special privileges — not even root inside a container — making it ideal for post-exploitation after any initial access. Because the page cache is shared across containers and the host, a compromised container can escalate to root on the entire node. Metasploit has shipped a local exploit module supporting AMD64 and AARCH64 architectures. Immediate mitigation: `echo "install algif_aead /bin/false" > /etc/modprobe.d/disable-algif-aead.conf && rmmod algif_aead`. [[Microsoft Defender Research](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/); [Rapid7 Metasploit Wrap-Up](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/pt-metasploit-wrap-up-05-01-2026)]

## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[UPDATE] Cordial Spider and Snarky Spider weaponize AiTM phishing for high-speed SaaS data theft**

New technical details have emerged on the Scattered Spider-affiliated groups profiled yesterday. CrowdStrike's Falcon Shield telemetry reveals that both groups begin with voice phishing — impersonating internal IT support to manufacture urgency — then direct victims to adversary-in-the-middle phishing pages that perfectly clone corporate SSO portals. Once session tokens are captured, the attackers register their own MFA devices (CORDIAL SPIDER uses Windows Quick Emulator, SNARKY SPIDER prefers Genymobile Android emulators), delete existing legitimate MFA devices, and create inbox rules that automatically trash security alert emails containing keywords like "alert," "incident," "MFA," and "security warning." SNARKY SPIDER begins data exfiltration within one hour of initial compromise. The groups specifically hunt for SSNs, financial contracts, VPN credentials, and confidential business documents across SharePoint, HubSpot, and Google Workspace. Commercial VPN services (Mullvad, Oxylabs, NetNut) and residential proxy networks disguise traffic as legitimate residential internet activity. The attacks exploit customer misconfigurations — overly permissive access controls and lack of phishing-resistant MFA — rather than software vulnerabilities. Organizations using SaaS platforms should enforce phishing-resistant MFA, monitor for MFA device enrollment anomalies, and audit inbox rules for security keyword suppression. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/aitm-login-pages-fuel-attacks-on-sharepoint/)]

**[NEW] "AccountDumpling" Facebook phishing operation compromises 30,000+ business accounts using Google AppSheet for authenticated email delivery**

Guardio Labs has uncovered a Vietnamese-linked phishing operation that has compromised more than 30,000 Facebook accounts, primarily targeting business account owners in the United States and Europe. The campaign's distinguishing feature is its abuse of Google AppSheet to send fully authenticated phishing emails — because messages originate from legitimate Google infrastructure, they pass SPF, DKIM, and DMARC checks and bypass secure email gateways entirely. The operators developed four distinct attack clusters: the primary cluster directed victims to Netlify-hosted pages cloning the Facebook Help Center, harvesting credentials plus identity documents including dates of birth and government ID photos. A secondary cluster offered fake blue badge verifications through Vercel-hosted pages that intercepted MFA codes in real time using invisible Unicode characters to evade NLP-based detection. A third cluster used Google Drive to host Canva-crafted fake Meta notification PDFs linking to a Socket.IO-based phishing panel with live WebSocket traffic, enabling human operators to request specific 2FA codes and capture browser screenshots. A fourth cluster impersonated corporate recruiters from major tech brands to build trust before moving targets to off-platform channels. All stolen data was streamed in real time via Telegram bots to private channels for rapid account takeover. Attribution to a Vietnam-based operator was confirmed through Google Drive PDF metadata and Vietnamese developer comments embedded in malicious JavaScript. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/massive-facebook-phishing-operation/)]

**[NEW] MacSync stealer delivered via 200+ fake Google Ads impersonating Homebrew, 7-Zip, and other popular macOS software**

A widespread malvertising campaign has been observed using over 200 hijacked Google Ads accounts to distribute the MacSync credential stealer to macOS users. The ads impersonate popular software including Homebrew, 7-Zip, The Unarchiver, LibreOffice, Microsoft Office, OBS Studio, Final Cut Pro, and AppCleaner. Rather than directing victims to fake download portals, the ads redirect to publicly accessible Evernote notes containing step-by-step Terminal installation instructions — a technique that leverages Evernote's domain reputation to bypass filters. Victims are instructed to paste Base64-encoded commands that decode and execute shell scripts, which deploy MacSync v1.1.2. The malware establishes persistence, harvests browser cookies and login databases, steals data from Telegram, macOS Notes, and cryptocurrency wallets, and can display fake password prompts to capture system credentials. The campaign is part of the broader ClickFix ecosystem that has been evolving since November 2025, with CIS reporting active targeting of U.S. state and local government organizations. Users should never execute Terminal commands from web pages, and organizations should monitor for unusual `curl` piped to `bash` or `zsh` executions. [[Bitdefender](https://www.bitdefender.com/en-au/blog/hotforsecurity/criminals-use-fake-mac-homebrew-google-ads-in-new-malicious-campaign); [CIS](https://www.cisecurity.org/insights/blog/macsync-stealer-campaign-impacting-us-sltt-macos-users)]

## 📋 Policy & Industry News

**[NEW] Five Eyes agencies publish joint guidance on secure deployment of agentic AI systems**

CISA, NSA, and their counterparts in Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK have jointly published guidance urging organizations to treat autonomous AI systems as a core cybersecurity concern. The document focuses on agentic AI — LLM-based systems that can plan, make decisions, and take actions autonomously without human review at each stage. The agencies identify five risk categories: excessive privilege granting (a single compromise causing cascading damage), design/configuration flaws, behavioral risks where agents pursue goals in unintended ways, structural risks across interconnected agent networks, and accountability gaps where opaque decision-making makes incident forensics difficult. Key recommendations include cryptographically secured identities for each agent, short-lived credentials, mandatory human sign-off for high-impact actions, and treating prompt injection as a first-class threat. The agencies warn that until security practices mature, organizations should "assume that agentic AI systems may behave unexpectedly" and prioritize resilience and reversibility over efficiency gains. [[CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/cisa-nsa-five-eyes-guidance-secure-deployment-ai-agents/)]

**[NEW] Two former ransomware negotiators sentenced to four years each for running their own ransomware attacks**

Ryan Goldberg (Georgia) and Kevin Martin (Texas) each received four-year prison sentences after pleading guilty to conspiracy to obstruct interstate commerce by extortion. A third defendant, Angelo Martino (Florida), pleaded guilty with sentencing scheduled for July 9. All three were employed at cybersecurity firms — two specifically as ransomware negotiators — when they pivoted to conducting attacks themselves using the BlackCat/Alphv ransomware. The case represents a deliberate corruption of trusted defensive roles and underscores insider risk within the incident response and negotiation ecosystem. **[NEW] Google overhauls bug bounty programs as AI-driven vulnerability flood overwhelms triage**

Google has restructured its Vulnerability Reward Programs in response to a flood of AI-assisted security research that has overwhelmed vulnerability triage — the Internet Bug Bounty program recently paused accepting new reports entirely due to the volume. Chrome bug payouts are dropping significantly: base rewards for memory safety issues are now $500 (with multipliers), and some researchers report rewards 10x smaller than before. Android rewards are increasing, with zero-click Pixel Titan M exploits now up to $1.5 million. Google is shifting both programs to prioritize concrete proof-of-exploitation over lengthy write-ups, explicitly noting that AI tools now make detailed reports effortless to generate while internal tooling can explain bugs automatically. Despite lower per-bug Chrome payouts, Google expects to increase total aggregate rewards for 2026 after paying a record $17.1 million in 2025. [[SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/google-adjusts-bug-bounties-chrome-payouts-drop-as-android-rewards-rise-amid-ai-surge/)]
