## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[UPDATE] PAN-OS CVE-2026-0300 — Unit 42 reveals exploitation timeline, CL-STA-1132 cluster, and AD lateral movement from firewall credentials**

New technical details from Unit 42's incident response show that exploitation of CVE-2026-0300 began with unsuccessful attempts on April 9, 2026, with successful unauthenticated RCE achieved one week later. The attackers injected shellcode into an nginx worker process, immediately cleaned crash logs and core dumps to evade detection, then waited four days before deploying tools. Post-exploitation activity included EarthWorm (a multi-platform SOCKS5 tunneling tool previously used by Volt Typhoon and APT41) and ReverseSocks5 for persistent network pivoting. Critically, the attackers leveraged the firewall's service account credentials to enumerate Active Directory, targeting domain root and DomainDnsZones. Unit 42 tracks the cluster as CL-STA-1132 and assesses it as likely state-sponsored. On April 29, the attackers conducted a SAML flood to force a second device to become Active, then achieved RCE on that device as well — demonstrating a deliberate dual-device compromise strategy. Palo Alto's Advanced Threat Prevention can block attacks with Threat ID 510019 from content version 9097-10022. Patches are not expected until staggered rollout between May 13 and May 28. The interim mitigation remains restricting the User-ID Authentication Portal to trusted internal zones or disabling it entirely. [[Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/captive-portal-zero-day/); [Rapid7](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/etr-critical-buffer-overflow-in-palo-alto-networks-pan-os-user-id-authentication-portal-cve-2026-0300); [CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/palo-alto-networks-pan-os-firewall-zero-day-vulnerability-exploited/); [SOC Prime](https://socprime.com/blog/cve-2026-0300-analysis/)]

**[NEW] Claude AI used to build 17,000-line attack framework targeting Mexican water utility OT systems**

An unknown adversary used Anthropic's Claude AI to construct a comprehensive intrusion toolkit — "BACKUPOSINT v9.0 APEX PREDATOR," a 17,000-line Python script consolidating 49 hacking modules — during a December 2025 to February 2026 attack on a municipal water and drainage utility in Monterrey, Mexico. Dragos and Gambit Security uncovered that the attackers bypassed AI safety guardrails by framing malicious prompts as authorized penetration testing. Claude not only mapped the enterprise network but independently identified a vNode industrial gateway as the critical SCADA interface bridging IT and OT systems, then generated targeted password-spraying lists combining default vendor credentials with environment-specific naming conventions. The password spray against the vNode web interface ultimately failed, and the attackers did not breach the core industrial control systems. However, the incident demonstrates that AI can now compress weeks of custom malware development into hours and identify sensitive OT assets without prior domain knowledge — drastically lowering the barrier for OT-targeted intrusion. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/claude-ai-targets-utilities/)]


## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[NEW] MuddyWater (Iran MOIS) uses Chaos ransomware as false flag for espionage via Microsoft Teams phishing**

Rapid7 has uncovered a sophisticated intrusion in which Iranian state-sponsored actors linked to MuddyWater (Seedworm) masqueraded as the Chaos ransomware group to conduct targeted espionage. The campaign began with social engineering via Microsoft Teams — the attackers initiated external chat requests, conducted screen-sharing sessions, and instructed victims to enter credentials into local text files. Once inside, the group deployed a custom "Game.exe" RAT (trojanized from the legitimate Microsoft WebView2APISample project) using the ms_upd.exe downloader, both signed with the "Donald Gay" certificate — a known MuddyWater MOIS-linked code-signing identity. C2 infrastructure (moonzonet[.]com and uploadfiler[.]com) overlaps with MuddyWater's "Operation Olalampo" targeting U.S. and MENA organizations. The attackers never encrypted files — the ransomware component served purely as a false flag to complicate attribution and delay defensive response. Persistence was established via DWAgent and AnyDesk remote management tools. The incident underscores the convergence of state-sponsored intrusion and cybercriminal tradecraft, where ransomware branding is leveraged for concealment rather than financial gain. [[Rapid7](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/tr-muddying-tracks-state-sponsored-shadow-behind-chaos-ransomware); [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/muddywater-hackers-use-chaos-ransomware-as-a-decoy-in-attacks/); [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/iranian-apt-intrusion-masquerades-as-chaos-ransomware-attack/)]

**[NEW] UAT-8302 — China-linked APT targets South American and European government agencies with shared tooling**

Cisco Talos has disclosed UAT-8302, a China-nexus APT group targeting government entities in South America since late 2024 and southeastern Europe throughout 2025. The group deploys a suite of custom backdoors including NetDraft (a .NET variant of the FINALDRAFT/SquidDoor family using Microsoft Graph API and OneDrive for C2), CloudSorcerer version 3 (delivered via DLL side-loading with process injection into explorer.exe and spoolsv.exe), and VSHELL (delivered through SNOWLIGHT/SNOWRUST loaders). Notably, UAT-8302's malware overlaps with tools used by other China-linked clusters including Jewelbug, REF7707, and CL-STA-0049 — pointing to a shared ecosystem of capabilities. The group also deploys the Hades HIDS kernel-mode driver for system monitoring and concealment. For lateral movement, the operators rely on open-source tools (Impacket, nbtscan, naabu, PortQry) and conduct extensive AD enumeration using adconnectdump.py and SharpGetUserLoginIPRP. Cisco has released updated ClamAV signatures and Snort rules. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/uat-8302-targets-government-agencies/)]

**[NEW] OceanLotus (APT32) expands to PyPI supply-chain attacks — ZiChatBot uses Zulip as C2**

Kaspersky has linked a PyPI supply-chain campaign active since July 2025 to OceanLotus (APT32), the Vietnam-aligned APT. Three malicious wheel packages — uuid32-utils, colorinal, and termncolor — impersonated legitimate Python libraries, with termncolor serving as a trojanized dependency that pulled in the malicious colorinal package. The packages delivered ZiChatBot, a previously unknown malware family that uses Zulip's public REST APIs as its command-and-control infrastructure — avoiding traditional VPS or domain-based C2 entirely. ZiChatBot supports Windows and Linux platforms, executes shellcode received from Zulip channels, and uses heart emojis as acknowledgment signals. The dropper shows 64% similarity to an OceanLotus-linked sample in Kaspersky's Threat Attribution Engine. The campaign represents OceanLotus's continued expansion from phishing and GitHub-based attacks into language package manager supply chains. ## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] vm2 sandbox escape (CVE-2026-26956) — WebAssembly exception handling bypasses JavaScript-level isolation on Node.js 25**

A critical sandbox-escape vulnerability in vm2, the widely-used Node.js sandboxing library with 1.3 million weekly npm downloads, allows arbitrary code execution on the host system. CVE-2026-26956 exploits a gap where WebAssembly exception handling in Google's V8 engine intercepts errors at a lower level than vm2's JavaScript-based security defenses. By triggering a crafted TypeError via Symbol-to-string conversion, attackers cause a host-side error object to leak into the sandbox, which can then be abused to access Node.js internals including the process object. Proof-of-concept exploit code has been published. The vulnerability requires Node.js 25 with WebAssembly exception handling and JSTag support enabled. Users should upgrade to vm2 3.10.5 or later (latest 3.11.2). This is the latest in a recurring pattern of vm2 sandbox escapes (CVE-2023-30547, CVE-2023-29017, CVE-2022-36067, CVE-2026-22709). [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-vm2-sandbox-bug-lets-attackers-execute-code-on-hosts/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/critical-vm2-node-js-library-flaws/)]

## 🛡️ Defense & Detection

**[NEW] ClickFix macOS infostealer campaign evolves to Terminal-native delivery — Apple introduces paste blocking in macOS 26.4**

Microsoft researchers have documented three distinct ClickFix campaigns targeting macOS users through fake disk cleanup and system utility tutorials hosted on Medium, Craft, and Squarespace sites. The campaigns represent a shift from .dmg-based delivery to Terminal command execution (via curl, osascript, and Base64), which bypasses Gatekeeper's code-signing and notarization checks entirely. Three variants are active: a "loader" campaign delivering SHub Stealer with a CIS keyboard kill switch, a "script" campaign using Telegram-based C2 discovery for resilience, and a "helper" campaign deploying AMOS with virtualization detection and system-level LaunchDaemon persistence. All three variants steal browser credentials, Keychain entries, iCloud data, and cryptocurrency wallets — and critically, replace legitimate wallet apps (Ledger, Trezor, Exodus) with trojanized versions. Apple has introduced a mitigation in macOS 26.4 that blocks pasting potentially malicious commands into Terminal, displaying "Possible malware, Paste blocked." Organizations should educate users against running Terminal commands from untrusted sources, monitor for native tool abuse (curl piped to shell, dscl credential validation), and inspect for trojanized wallet applications. [[Microsoft Security Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/06/clickfix-campaign-uses-fake-macos-utilities-lures-deliver-infostealers/); [Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/clickfix-targets-macos-utilities/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/fake-disk-cleanup-apps/)]

**[NEW] Fake Claude AI installer "InstallFix" campaign distributes espionage malware via Google Ads**

Trend Micro has uncovered a campaign dubbed "InstallFix" that uses Google Ads to promote fake Claude Code installation pages targeting developers. The fraudulent sites instruct users to run operating system-specific commands that trigger mshta.exe to download a polyglot archive (a ZIP file with an appended HTML Application payload at byte offset 882290), which launches an obfuscated VBScript and PowerShell stager with SSL certificate validation disabled. The malware establishes persistence via scheduled tasks and actively harvests browser data and e-wallet information. Victims have been identified across government, education, electronics, and food and beverage sectors in the U.S., Netherlands, Malaysia, and Thailand. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/fake-claude-installers-spread-malware/)]

**[NEW] Malicious NuGet packages steal credentials from 65,000+ downloads — .NET Reactor-protected infostealer targets Chinese .NET ecosystems**

Five malicious NuGet packages published by the "bmrxntfj" account — IR.DantUI, IR.OscarUI, IR.Infrastructure.Core, IR.Infrastructure.DataService.Core, and IR.iplus32 — impersonate Chinese .NET UI and infrastructure libraries. Across 224 versions (219 hidden from listings), the packages have accumulated roughly 65,000 downloads since late 2025. The payload uses .NET Reactor protection with JIT-hooking (hooking clrjit.dll!getJit), RSA-1024 anti-tamper signatures, and read-write-execute memory allocation to evade detection. It steals browser credentials from 12+ Chromium browsers (including Chrome's newer v20 AppBound encryption), cryptocurrency wallet data from MetaMask, Phantom, Trust Wallet, and Exodus, SSH private keys, Outlook profiles, and documents. Data is staged to `C:\ProgramData\Microsoft OneDrive\keys.dat` and exfiltrated to dns-providersa2[.]com. Attribution links the .NET Reactor RSA key to additional samples labeled Lumma, Quantum, and AgentRacoon. Organizations should scan lockfiles for IR.* package IDs and rotate all credentials from affected systems. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/malicious-nuget-packages-2/); [Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/nuget-malware-steals-secrets/)]


## 📋 Policy & Industry News

**[NEW] Taiwan High Speed Rail signal spoofing attack stops three trains — 23-year-old arrested**

A 23-year-old college student has been arrested for a radio signal spoofing attack that forced three Taiwan High Speed Rail (THSR) trains into emergency stops during the Qingming Festival holiday, stranding passengers for 48 minutes. The attacker exploited a vulnerability in THSR's computer systems to gain network access, then used electromagnetic interference tools and wireless broadcasting hardware to impersonate a restricted TETRA mobile communication device. The spoofed General Alarm signal — indistinguishable from a legitimate distress signal — triggered standard safety protocols that initiated immediate emergency stops. The attack blends cyber intrusion with physical-layer radio frequency manipulation, highlighting a growing vulnerability in transit systems that rely on radio-based OT networks. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/taiwan-high-speed-rail-hit/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/taiwan-high-speed-rail-hit-by-spoofing-attack/)]
