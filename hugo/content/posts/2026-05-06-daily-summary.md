## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[NEW] Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS zero-day (CVE-2026-0300) — unauthenticated RCE with root on firewalls, actively exploited, no patch for 7+ days**

Palo Alto Networks has disclosed a critical buffer overflow vulnerability in the PAN-OS User-ID Authentication Portal (Captive Portal) that is being actively exploited in attacks. Tracked as CVE-2026-0300 with a CVSS 4.0 score of 9.3, the flaw allows unauthenticated remote attackers to execute arbitrary code with root privileges by sending specially crafted packets to exposed firewalls. PA-Series and VM-Series appliances running PAN-OS 10.2, 11.1, 11.2, and 12.1 are affected. Shadowserver is tracking over 5,800 internet-exposed VM-Series firewalls, with the largest concentrations in Asia (2,466) and North America (1,998). Palo Alto Networks describes exploitation as "limited" — a phrasing that historically indicates targeted state-sponsored operations. Patches are not expected until a staggered rollout between May 13 and May 28, 2026. Until then, the only mitigation is restricting the User-ID Authentication Portal to trusted internal network zones or disabling it entirely. Prisma Access, Cloud NGFW, and Panorama appliances are not affected. Palo Alto firewalls are used by over 70,000 organizations including 90% of Fortune 10 companies and most major U.S. banks. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/palo-alto-networks-warns-of-actively-exploited-firewall-zero-day/); [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/palo-alto-networks-to-patch-zero-day-exploited-to-hack-firewalls/); [Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/critical-palo-alto-firewall-flaw-exploited-to-gain-root-access/)]

**[NEW] DAEMON Tools trojanized in active supply-chain attack — digitally signed installers deliver backdoor to thousands across 100+ countries**

A sophisticated supply-chain attack has compromised DAEMON Tools installers (versions 12.5.0.2421 through 12.5.0.2434), specifically the DTHelper.exe, DiscSoftBusServiceLite.exe, and DTShellHlp.exe binaries. Since April 8, thousands of systems across more than 100 countries downloaded the trojanized software from the official DAEMON Tools website. The attack evaded detection for nearly a month. Kaspersky reports that while first-stage infections are widespread, second-stage payloads were deployed to only about a dozen machines — indicating a highly targeted operation focused on high-value victims in retail, scientific, government, and manufacturing sectors in Russia, Belarus, and Thailand. String analysis suggests a Chinese-speaking threat actor. The first-stage payload acts as an information stealer (hostname, MAC, processes, locale) for victim profiling, while qualifying targets receive a lightweight backdoor capable of in-memory code execution. In at least one case, the advanced QUIC RAT multi-protocol implant was deployed against a Russian educational institution. Organizations should audit any machines with DAEMON Tools installed on or after April 8 for abnormal activity, and verify software integrity against known-good hashes. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/daemon-tools-trojanized-in-supply-chain-attack-to-deploy-backdoor/); [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/government-scientific-entities-hit-via-daemon-tools-supply-chain-attack/)]

**[NEW] Axios npm supply-chain compromise delivered cross-platform RAT to anyone who installed during 3-hour window** [r/cybersecurity]

Two malicious versions of axios — the JavaScript HTTP client with over 100 million weekly npm downloads — were published on March 31 after the lead maintainer's PC was compromised through a targeted social engineering campaign and RAT malware. Versions 1.14.1 and 0.30.4 injected a phantom dependency, `plain-crypto-js@4.2.1`, whose `postinstall` hook deployed a cross-platform remote access trojan targeting Windows, Linux, and macOS with platform-specific payloads. The compromise window was brief (00:21–03:29 UTC), but any CI/CD pipeline, developer workstation, or container build that ran `npm install` during that window should be treated as fully compromised. The attacker changed the maintainer's account email to a ProtonMail address and published directly via npm CLI, bypassing the project's GitHub Actions OIDC-signed pipeline. The malicious versions were published without provenance attestations — a detectable anomaly. The RAT beaconed to `sfrclak[.]com:8000` (IP: `142.11.206.73`) and harvested browser sessions, SSH keys, cloud credentials, and shell history. Safe versions: axios 1.14.0 (1.x line) or 0.30.3 (0.x line). This incident is resurfacing on Reddit this week as organizations continue discovering affected lockfiles. [[GitHub Issue #10636](https://github.com/axios/axios/issues/10636); [Snyk](https://snyk.io/blog/axios-npm-package-compromised-supply-chain-attack-delivers-cross-platform/); [Malwarebytes](https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/news/2026/03/axios-supply-chain-attack-chops-away-at-npm-trust)]

**[NEW] DarkSword iOS exploit kit chains six zero-days to fully compromise iPhones — now leaked to broader abuse**

Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) has disclosed DarkSword, a full-chain iOS exploit kit that leverages six zero-day vulnerabilities to compromise devices running iOS 18.4 through 18.7. Three distinct malware families have been deployed: GHOSTBLADE, GHOSTKNIFE, and GHOSTSABER. Since November 2025, multiple commercial surveillance vendors and suspected state-sponsored actors have used DarkSword against targets in Saudi Arabia, Turkey, Malaysia, and Ukraine. Notably, UNC6353 — a suspected Russian espionage group previously linked to the Coruna iOS exploit kit — has incorporated DarkSword into watering-hole attacks. A week after initial identification, a version of the exploit leaked online, significantly expanding the pool of potential abusers. The proliferation pattern — from government/commercial surveillance to leaked public tooling — mirrors prior iOS exploit kits like Coruna and highlights the limited shelf-life of zero-day exclusivity. All affected iOS versions have since been patched; devices running current iOS releases are not at risk. [[Schneier on Security](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/05/darksword-malware.html)]

## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[NEW] Iran-linked operators breach 11 Omani government entities via exposed webshells and ProxyShell**

Iran-aligned threat operators have conducted an extensive espionage campaign against Omani government networks, compromising at least 11 entities including the Ministry of Justice and Legal Affairs, Royal Oman Police, Royal Fleet of Oman, and the Tax Authority. The operation was exposed through a poorly secured C2 server — a RouterHosting VPS in the UAE — that hosted the full toolkit including ASP.NET webshells, Python exploit scripts, Chisel tunneling binaries, and exfiltrated data. Attackers leveraged ProxyShell exploitation (CVE-2021-34473/34523/31207) against Exchange servers and password brute-force attacks against government portals. The primary target, the Ministry of Justice, suffered large-scale data theft including 26,596 DotNetNuke user records with staff credentials, Windows SAM and SYSTEM registry hives, and judicial case data. National ID records containing citizen names, birthdates, and nationality data were also extracted. The campaign's infrastructure — spread across a "dubai-#" cluster on the same ASN and linked to cloned Iranian diaspora media sites — strongly suggests a Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS) nexus. Organizations should audit for the provided IOCs and review Exchange server patching status. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/iran-linked-hackers-target-oman-ministries/)]

**[NEW] QLNX Linux RAT targets developers for supply-chain credential theft — rootkit, PAM backdoors, and eBPF hiding**

Trend Micro has documented QLNX, a previously undocumented Linux remote access trojan purpose-built for supply-chain compromise. The 64-bit ELF binary executes from anonymous memory (memfd_create), spoofs its process name as a kernel thread ("kworker/0:0", "ksoftirqd/0", etc.), and deploys both userland and eBPF-based rootkits to hide files, directories, processes, and network ports. QLNX ships with embedded C source code compiled on-target for a PAM backdoor that harvests plaintext credentials from all authentication flows and installs a master password ("OfQtYJK"), plus an LD_PRELOAD rootkit hooking libc functions. Its credential harvesting sweeps SSH keys, browser data, cloud credentials (AWS, Kubernetes, Docker), developer tooling secrets (.npmrc, .pypirc, .git-credentials, GitHub CLI tokens, Vault tokens, Terraform credentials), and system password hashes — everything needed to hijack npm/PyPI publishing accounts, CI/CD pipelines, and cloud infrastructure. The malware supports 58+ command IDs, P2P mesh networking for C2 resilience, and seven persistence mechanisms. Its design directly supports supply-chain attacks like the LiteLLM and Axios npm compromises. Anomalous "kernel" processes on developer workstations, unexpected entries in /etc/ld.so.preload, and unexplained GCC compilation activity should be treated as high-priority IOC. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/qlnx-targets-developers-in-supply-chain/)]

The malware plays a silent MP3 loop to prevent process suspension. Windows victims on the same platform receive a trojanized DLL (mono.dll) that deploys RokRAT, which then loads the Windows version of BirdCall. ## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] Argo CD critical auth bypass (CVE-2026-42880) exposes Kubernetes Secrets from etcd — CVSS 9.6**

A critical vulnerability in Argo CD allows any authenticated user with read-only access to extract plaintext Kubernetes Secrets from the etcd datastore, including service account tokens, database credentials, API keys, and TLS certificates. Tracked as CVE-2026-42880 with a CVSS score of 9.6, the flaw exists in the ServerSideDiff endpoint, which fails to apply data-masking consistently. While other endpoints (GetManifests, PatchResource) properly strip secret values, ServerSideDiff returns raw, unmasked states when applications use the `compare-options` annotation with mutation webhooks enabled. The vulnerability affects Argo CD versions 3.2.0 through 3.3.8. A proof-of-concept exploit has been demonstrated. Patched versions 3.2.11 and 3.3.9 are available. Organizations running Argo CD in production should upgrade immediately and audit access logs for any unusual Secret retrieval patterns during the exposure window. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/argo-cd-serversidediff-flaw/)]

**[NEW] Schemata DoD contractor zero-auth flaw exposed military training data and service member base deployments for 150 days**

A critical authorization vulnerability in Schemata, an AI-powered virtual training platform backed by Andreessen Horowitz with active DoD contracts, allowed unprivileged accounts to access sensitive military training materials and personnel records across all tenant boundaries. Security researcher Alex Shapiro, using the open-source AI hacking agent Strix, discovered that the platform's API lacked any meaningful organizational scoping or permission checks — returning the entire platform's data regardless of the requesting user's tenant. Exposed data included the complete user directory with full names, emails, and enrollment data, as well as the specific military base deployments of active U.S. service members, metadata and direct AWS S3 links for confidential naval maintenance training materials, and restricted Army field manuals on explosive ordnance deployment. Write-enabled routes lacked authorization checks entirely, meaning attackers could have manipulated or deleted critical training infrastructure. Despite being contacted on December 2, 2025, Schemata did not patch until May 1, 2026 — 150 days later — after initially dismissing the report as a bug bounty solicitation. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/zero-auth-vulnerability-dod-contractor/)]

**[NEW] Ollama "Bleeding Llama" (CVE-2026-7482) exposes 300,000 deployments to unauthenticated heap data theft**

Cyera researchers have disclosed a critical heap out-of-bounds read vulnerability in Ollama, the open-source local LLM runtime, tracked as CVE-2026-7482 with a CVSS score of 9.3. The flaw exists in the GGUF model loader and is triggered by processing a malicious model file with declared tensor offsets exceeding the actual file size, causing the server to read past the allocated heap buffer. An attacker can then exfiltrate the resulting file — containing stolen heap data — to a remote server using Ollama's built-in model push feature. The entire attack requires only three unauthenticated API calls. Ollama's default configuration launches without authentication and listens on all interfaces, meaning approximately 300,000 internet-exposed deployments are immediately exploitable. Stolen heap data may contain environment variables, API keys, tokens, developer prompts, code, and PII/PHI. Organizations should update to Ollama 0.17.1, restrict network access with firewalls, and place authentication proxies in front of deployments. Any instance previously accessible from the internet should be treated as compromised. [[SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/critical-bug-could-expose-300000-ollama-deployments-to-information-theft/)]

**[NEW] n8n unauthenticated RCE (CVE-2026-42231) — xml2js prototype pollution escalates to full shell access**

A prototype pollution vulnerability in the xml2js XML parsing library has been chained into unauthenticated remote code execution in n8n, the open-source workflow automation platform. The vulnerability stems from a CoffeeScript-to-JavaScript compilation semantic difference that allows XML tags matching inherited Object.prototype properties to inject values into parsed objects. An attacker sending a crafted XML payload to any configured webhook endpoint can achieve prototype pollution, which is then escalated to RCE through the n8n Git node's handling of the `GIT_SSH_COMMAND` environment variable. The exploit requires no authentication and no custom plugins — only the default Git node. Self-hosted n8n instances with publicly accessible webhook endpoints are at immediate risk. Organizations using n8n should apply the patched version immediately and restrict webhook endpoint exposure. [[Malware.News/DeXpose](https://malware.news/t/n8n-from-parsing-bug-to-remote-code-execution-aka-cve-2026-42231/106681#post_1)]

## 🛡️ Defense & Detection

**[UPDATE] Azure AD Conditional Access bypass — phantom device registration, PRT theft, and Intune compliance spoofing detailed**

New technical details have emerged on the Howler Cell (Cyderes) red team exercise that demonstrated a complete Azure AD tenant compromise starting from a single credential. The full attack chain begins at the Device Registration Service (DRS) endpoint, which lacks enforcement-mode Conditional Access protection. Attackers registered a "phantom device" — a signed Azure AD certificate and private key generated on a standard Linux laptop with no TPM, no physical hardware, and no administrative approval. This mirrors Storm-2372 (Russian state-aligned) tradecraft documented since August 2024. Once registered, the attackers generated a Primary Refresh Token (PRT) stored in plaintext, granting silent access to cloud resources. Intune MDM compliance was bypassed by declaring fake hybrid domain-joined membership — Intune interpreted missing security feature responses (BitLocker, Secure Boot) as "not applicable" rather than "non-compliant," marking the attacker's Linux laptop as a fully compliant corporate device. The decrypted Intune management extension package exposed internal server hostnames, administrative shares, and network architecture. Critical enabler: multiple Conditional Access policies designed to block device code flows were left in **Report-Only mode**, logging every malicious action without blocking any. Defenders must immediately shift critical CA policies from Report-Only to enforcement, validate self-reported device compliance with hardware attestation, and audit hybrid domain-join configurations. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/azure-ad-security-bypass-exploits-phantom-device-registration-and-prt-abuse/); [Malware.News](https://malware.news/t/one-password-no-device-full-tenant-dismantling-azure-ad-conditional-access-through-device-identity-abuse/106669#post_1)]

**[NEW] Remus infostealer evolves from Lumma — bypasses App-Bound Encryption with 51-byte shellcode, uses EtherHiding for C2**

Gen Digital researchers have documented Remus, a 64-bit information-stealing malware representing the next evolution of the Lumma Stealer lineage. Following the public doxxing of alleged Lumma core members in late 2025, the malware was first seen as "Tenzor" test builds in September 2025 before entering active circulation as Remus in February 2026. The most significant capability is its bypass of Chromium Application-Bound Encryption: Remus injects a custom 51-byte shellcode directly into the browser process memory to hunt for the protected `v20_master_key`, enabling extraction of stored passwords, cookies, and autofill data. Remus also replaces Lumma's traditional dead-drop resolvers with EtherHiding — using Ethereum smart contracts hosted on the blockchain to resolve C2 server URLs, making infrastructure practically immune to standard takedown requests. The malware incorporates rigorous anti-analysis checks, scanning for sandbox and honeypot indicators, with silent self-termination if detected. IOCs include four C2 IP addresses on the `217.156.122[.]x` range and `45.151.106.110`. [[Cyber Security News](https://cyberpress.org/remus-bypasses-browser-encryption/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/remus-infostealer-adopts-lumma/)]

**[NEW] CloudZ RAT's Pheno plugin hijacks Microsoft Phone Link to intercept SMS and OTPs without compromising mobile devices**

Cisco Talos has discovered a new plugin called Pheno for the CloudZ remote access tool that abuses Microsoft Phone Link's local SQLite database to steal SMS messages and one-time passwords directly from the Windows host. The malware does not need to compromise the mobile device — it monitors for active Phone Link sessions and reads the synchronized message database on the PC. The infection begins with a fake ScreenConnect update that drops a Rust-based loader, followed by a .NET loader with anti-analysis checks (sandbox detection, VM checks, Wireshark/Fiddler/Procmon/Sysmon hunting). CloudZ rotates between three hardcoded user-agent strings and includes anti-caching headers to prevent proxy/CDN caching of C2 details. The intrusion was active since at least January 2026. Organizations should phase out SMS-based OTP in favor of phishing-resistant authenticators and hardware security keys. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cloudz-malware-abuses-microsoft-phone-link-to-steal-sms-and-otps/)]
