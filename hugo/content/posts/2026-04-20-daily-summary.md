# Daily Threat Intel Digest - 2026-04-20

## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[NEW] Vercel breach via Context.ai OAuth compromise exposes customer environment variables**
Vercel has confirmed a security breach stemming from the compromise of Context.ai, a third-party AI tool used by employees, granting attackers access to internal systems through a hijacked Google Workspace OAuth application. The attackers accessed customer environment variables not marked as "sensitive," potentially exposing API keys, tokens, and database credentials across a limited subset of customers who have been notified. The malicious OAuth App ID `110671459871-30f1spbu0hptbs60cb4vsmv79i7bbvqj.apps.googleusercontent.com` has been provided as an indicator for organizations using Google Workspace to check their environments [[Vercel Confirms Data Breach](https://cyberpress.org/vercel-confirms-data-breach-hackers-claim-access-to-internal-systems/); [GitGuardian Analysis](https://blog.gitguardian.com/vercel-april-2026-incident-non-sensitive-environment-variables-need-investigation-too/)].

**[NEW] FakeWallet iOS malware campaign steals seed phrases from App Store apps**
A sophisticated cryptocurrency theft campaign has distributed 26 phishing apps through the Apple App Store, primarily targeting users in China, to distribute trojanized versions of popular wallets including MetaMask, Ledger, Trust Wallet, and Coinbase. The malware extracts recovery phrases and private keys through malicious library injections and presents phishing interfaces that autocomplete mnemonic words to appear legitimate, exfiltrating encrypted seed phrases via hardcoded C2 domains like `hxxps://iosfc[.]com/ledger/ios/Rsakeycatch.php`. Kaspersky has identified 26 infected IPA files with hashes including `4126348d783393dd85ede3468e48405d` and recommends users who installed crypto apps outside official channels to check for indicators of compromise [[Kaspersky Research](https://malware.news/t/fakewallet-crypto-stealer-spreading-through-ios-apps-in-the-app-store/106231#post_1)].

**[NEW] Notion public pages expose editor email addresses without authentication**
Security researchers have disclosed that public Notion pages expose full names, email addresses, and profile photos of all editors through an unauthenticated API endpoint (`/api/v3/syncRecordValuesMain`), enabling mass data collection for phishing and social engineering campaigns. The vulnerability was reported to Notion via HackerOne in July 2022 but classified as "informative" with no fix implemented, resurfacing after researchers demonstrated active exploitation risks. Notion has acknowledged the issue as unacceptable and is developing a solution, recommending users unpublish sensitive documents, limit editor counts, and review public pages until a patch is released [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/public-notion-pages-expose-profile-photos-and-email-addresses-of-editors/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/public-notion-pages-expose-editors-profile/)].

**[NEW] Microsoft-signed malware delivered via FUD Crypt MaaS platform**
A Malware-as-a-Service platform operating at `fudcrypt.net` is abusing Microsoft's Azure Trusted Signing infrastructure to deliver fully undetectable, polymorphic payloads with legitimate Authenticode certificates reading "Microsoft Identity Verification Root CA → Microsoft ID Verified CS AOC CA 01 → [payload]." The service offers monthly subscriptions ranging from $800 to $2,000 and provides 20 carrier profiles including ProtonVPN, Zoom, and CCleaner for DLL sideloading, with defense evasion stacks that kill AMSI, silence ETW, and masquerade as explorer.exe to bypass UAC [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/fud-crypt-signs-malware/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/microsoft-signed-malware/)].

## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[NEW] Iranian MOIS operates multiple hacktivist personas for coordinated campaigns**
Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS) has been linked to a coordinated cyber campaign hiding behind three hacktivist personas—Homeland Justice, Karma/KarmaBelow80, and Handala—rather than operating as independent groups as previously claimed. The operation uses interchangeable identities to conduct global cyber campaigns while maintaining plausible deniability, representing a significant attribution development for threat intelligence teams tracking Iranian state activity [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/irans-mois-tied/)].

**[UPDATE] ZionSiphon OT malware targeting Israeli water systems contains self-destruct flaw**
Following yesterday's reporting on ZionSiphon's design to sabotage Israeli water treatment and desalination facilities, Darktrace analysis reveals the malware is currently non-functional due to an XOR key mismatch in the `IsTargetCountry()` validation logic. The malware validates Israeli IP ranges (2.52.0.0–2.55.255.255, 79.176.0.0–79.191.255.255, 212.150.0.0–212.150.255.255) and checks for OT-related processes like "DesalPLC," "ROController," and "ChlorineCtrl," but the comparison always fails and triggers the `SelfDestruct()` routine instead of the chlorine dosing and hydraulic pressure sabotage payload. Darktrace assesses this is either a development build, prematurely deployed sample, or intentionally defanged test variant—the underlying architecture remains functional and would require only minor code correction to become operational [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/zionsiphon-hits-water-infrastructure/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/zionsiphon-hits-israeli-water/)].

**[NEW] JanaWare ransomware targets Turkish users via Adwind RAT with geofencing**
A targeted ransomware campaign dubbed "JanaWare" is specifically designed to infect systems in Turkey, using Adwind RAT to deliver Turkish-language ransom notes with contact instructions for qTox and Tor-based onion sites. The malware validates both language settings and external IP geolocation before payload execution, uses AES encryption with keys sent to C2 via Tor, and includes anti-forensic features including a `FilePumper` class that modifies file hashes on each infection. The campaign appears lower-profile than enterprise ransomware operations but has potentially remained active for years due to its narrow focus [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/janaware-targets-turkish-users/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/janaware-ransomware-attack/)].

**[NEW] ATHR platform automates AI-powered vishing campaigns at scale**
A new cybercrime-as-a-service platform called ATHR is selling for $4,000 plus 10% profit cut to automate telephone-oriented attack delivery (TOAD) campaigns, integrating AI-driven text-to-speech vishing agents, Asterisk WebRTC telephony, and real-time credential harvesting panels that sync live with victim phone calls. The platform includes notification mailers spoofing brands like Google and Coinbase, creates feedback loops for attackers to monitor callback rates and tweak campaigns in real time, and enables a single operator to run massive campaigns across multiple brands simultaneously [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/athr-drives-ai-vishing/)].

## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] AppDomain hijacking exploits Intel utility for stealthy malware execution**
Attackers are weaponizing a legitimate Intel-signed binary through AppDomainManager hijacking to execute stealthy malware by manipulating the .NET runtime environment. The campaign begins with phishing emails containing ZIP files that include the trusted Intel executable, a malicious configuration file, and an obfuscated .NET DLL—placing a specially crafted config file alongside the executable forces the CLR to load the malicious DLL before the application's normal code runs, effectively bypassing code-signing protections without modifying the trusted application [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/appdomain-hijacks-intel-utility/)].

**[NEW] Gh0st RAT distributed with CloverPlus adware in multi-payload campaign**
The Splunk Threat Research Team has identified a new campaign using a sophisticated obfuscated loader to deploy both Gh0st Remote Access Trojan and CloverPlus adware, providing attackers with long-term backdoor access while monetizing through unwanted browser modifications. The malware extracts two encrypted payloads from its resource section, uses `rundll32.exe` for execution, establishes persistence via Windows Run registry keys and Windows Remote Access service manipulation, and actively monitors for RDP sessions to capture credentials via `GetKeyState()` Windows API calls [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/gh0st-rat-meets-cloverplus/)].

**[NEW] CVE-2024-3721 in TBK DVRs exploited to spread Nexcorium Mirai variant**
Threat actors are actively exploiting CVE-2024-3721, an OS command injection vulnerability in TBK DVR-4104 and DVR-4216 devices, to deploy Nexcorium—a new Mirai-based botnet that uses custom HTTP header "X-Hacked-By: Nexus Team – Exploited By Erratic." The malware initiates Telnet brute-force attacks with hardcoded default passwords, leverages legacy CVE-2017-17215 to compromise Huawei devices, and establishes persistence through `/etc/inittab`, `/etc/rc.local`, systemd services, and cron jobs before deleting its executable to evade analysis [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/nexcorium-hits-tbk-dvrs/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/tbk-dvr-vulnerability/)].

**[NEW] Critical Gardyn smart garden vulnerabilities enable remote device takeover**
CISA has disclosed multiple critical vulnerabilities (CVSS 9.3) in Gardyn Home Kit smart gardening systems including CVE-2025-1242, CVE-2025-10681, CVE-2026-28766, and CVE-2026-32662, affecting the mobile app (before v2.11.0), cloud API (before v2.12.2026), and firmware. The flaws include OS command injection, cleartext transmission of sensitive data, hardcoded credentials, missing authentication for critical functions, and authorization bypass via user-controlled keys—enabling unauthenticated attackers to compromise devices and potentially pivot into broader networks [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/gardyn-smart-gardens-vulnerabilities/)].

**[NEW] iTerm2 SSH escape sequences enable arbitrary code execution**
A vulnerability in the popular iTerm2 macOS terminal emulator allows arbitrary code execution through SSH escape sequences triggered by simply reading a text file using commands like `cat`. The discovery, made in partnership with OpenAI, demonstrates that previously assumed-safe operations can cross into code execution territory in certain terminal environments [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/iterm2-flaw-turns-ssh-escape-sequences/)].

**[NEW] SEO poisoning campaign delivers RMM tools via fake TestDisk website**
Attackers are manipulating search engine results to push a malicious TestDisk data recovery tool installer that uses DLL sideloading with a legitimate Microsoft binary to deploy ScreenConnect remote monitoring software. The campaign targets users actively searching for recovery tools, making the lure particularly effective since victims are expecting to download legitimate software, and abuses legitimate RMM tools commonly found in enterprise environments to avoid triggering security detections [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/seo-attack-deploys-rmm/)].

**[UPDATE] Microsoft releases emergency out-of-band updates for Windows Server issues**
Following previous coverage of April 2026 security update problems, Microsoft has released emergency updates addressing KB5082063 installation failures on Windows Server 2025 and LSASS crashes causing domain controller restart loops. Windows Server 2025 OOB update KB5091157 addresses both issues, while updates for Windows Server 2022 (KB5091575), 2019 (KB5091573), and 2016 (KB5091572) address only the domain controller restart issue [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-releases-emergency-updates-to-fix-windows-server-issues/)].

## 📋 Policy & Industry News

**[NEW] NSA uses Anthropic's Mythos AI despite Pentagon blacklist designation**
The U.S. National Security Agency is reportedly among approximately 40 organizations globally granted access to Anthropic's restricted "Mythos" AI model for cybersecurity and offensive operations, despite Anthropic remaining on the Department of Defense blacklist following failed negotiations over unrestricted access to Claude models. Mythos provides rapid vulnerability detection, exploit path generation, automated code auditing, and accelerated patching recommendations for critical infrastructure—highlighting growing tensions between national security priorities and internal policy restrictions within the defense ecosystem [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/anthropics-mythos-despite-pentagon-blacklist/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/nsa-confirms-use-of-anthropics-mythos-blacklist/)].

**[NEW] NIST shifts NVD to risk-based model amid 263% surge in CVE submissions**
The National Institute of Standards and Technology has overhauled the National Vulnerability Database processing model, moving to selective "enrichment" of only high-priority vulnerabilities due to a 263% increase in CVE submissions between 2020-2025 and a record 42,000 vulnerabilities processed in 2025. Starting April 15, 2026, NIST will prioritize vulnerabilities in CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, flaws affecting federal government systems, and critical software under Executive Order 14028—processing these within one business day while assigning all other submissions "Lowest Priority" status with optional manual enrichment requests [[Cyberpress](https://cyberpress.org/nist-shifts-to-risk-based-nvd-model/)].

**[NEW] Senate extends surveillance powers until April 30 amid House chaos**
The U.S. Senate has extended surveillance authorities until April 30 following chaotic votes in the House, providing a temporary extension while broader debates over reauthorization continue [[SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/senate-extends-surveillance-powers-until-april-30-after-chaotic-votes-in-house/)].

**[NEW] GreyNoise research shows network traffic spikes predict vulnerability disclosures**
Analysis of 103 days of data reveals that roughly half of all activity surges detected by GreyNoise were followed by a vulnerability disclosure from the same vendor within three weeks, with median warning of nine days before public alerts. The research identified 104 distinct surges across 18 vendors including Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, Fortinet, and Ivanti—establishing that coordinated reconnaissance spikes often precede vulnerability disclosures, providing defenders with early warning to harden systems before public patches are available [[CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/greynoise-traffic-surge-early-warning-system-network-edge-device-vulnerabilities/)].

**[NEW] British hacker pleads guilty to $8 million cryptocurrency theft campaign**
Tyler Robert Buchanan, a 24-year-old Scottish national, has pleaded guilty to orchestrating cyberattacks against over a dozen U.S. companies, stealing at least $8 million in cryptocurrency. The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Central District of California announced the guilty plea following the multi-year investigation [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/british-hacker-admits-stealing-millions-in-virtual-currency/)].
