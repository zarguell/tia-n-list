# Daily Threat Intel Digest - April 4, 2026

## 🔴 Critical Threats & Active Exploitation

**[UPDATE] Axios supply chain compromise escalates into targeted social engineering against npm maintainers**
The North Korean Sapphire Sleet operation behind last week's Axios npm compromise was not an isolated incident but part of a scalable campaign to infiltrate the global JavaScript software supply chain. Threat actors are actively hunting top-tier Node.js and npm maintainers through highly coordinated social engineering offensives designed to compromise high-value developer accounts. The Axios breach itself deployed a cross-platform RAT dropper via a hidden dependency (`plain-crypto-js`), contacting C2 infrastructure at `sfrclak[.]com` to deliver OS-specific payloads before self-destructing to evade detection. The payload exfiltrates credentials and establishes remote management capabilities, meaning any secrets present on systems that ran `npm install` with the poisoned versions (1.14.1 or 0.30.4) should be treated as compromised. Downgrade immediately to axios@1.14.0 or axios@0.30.3 and hunt for connections to `142.11.206[.]73` across developer workstations and CI/CD systems. [[GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/hackers-launch-social-engineering-offensive-against-key-node-js/); [Zscaler ThreatLabz](https://www.zscaler.com/blogs/security-research/supply-chain-attacks-surge-march-2026); [Cisco Talos](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/axois-npm-supply-chain-incident/); [Tenable](https://www.tenable.com/blog/the-developer-credential-economy-exposure-data-is-the-new-front-line-in-the-supply-chain-war)]

**[NEW] Sophisticated job phishing campaigns bypass MFA to steal corporate Google and Facebook credentials**
Two advanced phishing kits impersonating Coca-Cola and Ferrari are exploiting the current high-unemployment environment to harvest credentials through fake recruiting portals. The Coca-Cola campaign is particularly dangerous: it draws a simulated Chrome browser window within the webpage, then dynamically relays MFA challenges between the victim and the real Google login in real-time. The kit polls the attacker's backend every three seconds to determine which verification screen (email, authenticator, SMS, or Google prompt) to serve based on the actual auth challenge Google presents to the attacker. It also explicitly rejects `@gmail.com` addresses, targeting corporate Google Workspace accounts specifically for lateral movement into enterprise environments. The Ferrari campaign casts a wider net, redirecting victims to a fake Facebook login to harvest credentials for identity theft and contact list abuse. Both campaigns leverage the legitimacy of OAuth-style "Continue with..." buttons to bypass user suspicion. [[Malwarebytes](https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/threat-intel/2026/04/that-dream-job-offer-from-coca-cola-or-ferrari-its-a-trap-for-your-passwords)]

**[NEW] FBI surveillance systems breached in suspected Chinese operation, exposing wiretap target phone numbers**
A suspected China-nexus threat actor compromised an unclassified FBI system used to store pen register and tap-and-trace surveillance data, likely exposing phone numbers of individuals being monitored by the bureau. The FBI identified anomalous activity on February 17 and determined that access was obtained through a compromised third party. The DOJ has classified this as a "major incident" under FISMA. Access to this metadata could allow foreign intelligence services to map U.S. surveillance targets, identify compromised assets, and counter counterintelligence operations. This breach follows the 2024 Salt Typhoon compromises of telecom lawful intercept systems and represents continued targeting of U.S. law enforcement surveillance infrastructure. [[Nextgov/FCW](https://www.nextgov.com/cybersecurity/2026/04/suspected-chinese-breach-fbi-system-exposed-surveillance-targets-phone-numbers/412612/); [DataBreaches.net](https://databreaches.net/2026/04/03/fbi-calls-breach-of-sensitive-agency-networks-a-major-incident/)]

**[NEW] LinkedIn secretly fingerprinting 6,000+ browser extensions on 1 billion users**
Microsoft's LinkedIn is running an undocumented JavaScript fingerprinting script that scans visitors' browsers for over 6,236 installed extensions and collects detailed device telemetry without user consent. BleepingComputer independently confirmed the script, which checks for extensions by attempting to access file resources associated with specific extension IDs—a known enumeration technique. The script also harvests CPU core count, memory, screen resolution, timezone, battery status, and audio context data. The "BrowserGate" investigation alleges LinkedIn uses this intelligence to map competitor customer bases (tracking over 200 competing sales tools), identify corporate targets, and send enforcement threats to users of third-party applications. LinkedIn acknowledged the scanning but stated it is used solely to detect extensions that violate terms of service and protect platform stability. Regardless of intent, this creates a massive repository of device fingerprints tied to real identities, employers, and job roles—exactly the data profile supply chain and social engineering operators seek. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/linkedin-secretely-scans-for-6-000-plus-chrome-extensions-collects-data/); [CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/linkedin-accused-of-secretly-checking-device/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/linkedin-hidden-code-secretly-scans-users-computers/)]

## 🎯 Threat Actor Activity & Campaigns

**[UPDATE] Qilin ransomware expands to German political party and Arkansas law enforcement**
The Qilin ransomware operation—already tracked for its EDR-blinding kernel drivers—has claimed two new high-profile victims: Germany's Die Linke political party and the Faulkner County Sheriff's Office in Arkansas. Die Linke confirmed that attackers stole data from internal party systems and may have obtained personal information of employees at party headquarters, though the membership database was not compromised. The party described the attack as potentially politically motivated hybrid warfare. The Faulkner County incident threatens exposure of sensitive law enforcement data. Both victims were added to Qilin's dark web leak site, signaling the group's continued aggressive expansion across both government and political sectors. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/die-linke-german-political-party-confirms-data-stolen-by-qilin-ransomware/); [DeXpose](https://www.dexpose.io/qilin-targets-faulkner-county-sheriffs-office-in-ransomware-attack/)]

**[NEW] Malicious AI agent skills weaponize autonomous coding assistants**
Threat actors are actively developing malicious "skills" for AI coding agents that silently exfiltrate AWS credentials during refactoring tasks, download payloads while summarizing documents, and perform other adversarial actions under the guise of legitimate assistant behavior. As agentic AI frameworks proliferate, these weaponized skills represent a new attack surface where the AI's autonomous execution capabilities are turned against the developer's environment. The implications are particularly severe for organizations that have granted AI agents broad filesystem and API access without robust permission boundaries. [[ThreatDown by Malwarebytes](https://www.threatdown.com/blog/weaponizing-autonomy-the-rise-of-malicious-ai-agent-skills/); [Palo Alto Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/amazon-bedrock-multiagent-applications/)]

**[UPDATE] TeamPCP supply chain campaign hits LiteLLM with secret-harvesting payloads**
The TeamPCP "chaos-as-a-service" operation previously targeting Trivy and KICS has also compromised LiteLLM, a popular Python AI infrastructure library with 3.4 million daily downloads. Two poisoned versions (1.82.7 and 1.82.8) were published on PyPI for approximately three hours before quarantine. Version 1.82.8 was particularly stealthy: it introduced a `.pth` file that Python automatically executes during startup on any invocation—even if LiteLLM isn't imported—while still passing pip's hash verification because it was published with legitimate credentials. The payloads targeted high-value secrets including AWS, GCP, Azure tokens, SSH keys, and Kubernetes credentials for lateral movement across CI/CD systems. Upgrade to version 1.82.6 (last known clean version) and rotate any credentials that may have been exposed. [[Zscaler ThreatLabz](https://www.zscaler.com/blogs/security-research/supply-chain-attacks-surge-march-2026); [Cisco Talos](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/protecting-supply-chain-2026/)]

## ⚠️ Vulnerabilities & Patches

**[NEW] Multi-agent AI systems on Amazon Bedrock expose new prompt injection attack surfaces**
Palo Alto Unit 42 research on Amazon Bedrock's multi-agent applications reveals architectural vulnerabilities where prompt injection against a single agent can cascade through the entire multi-agent orchestration. Attackers can manipulate task routing, inject malicious instructions into agent communication channels, and exploit the trust relationships between coordinating AI agents to exfiltrate data or perform unauthorized actions. Organizations deploying multi-agent AI frameworks should implement strict input sanitization at every agent boundary and avoid granting shared elevated permissions across agent clusters. [[Palo Alto Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/amazon-bedrock-multiagent-applications/)]

## 📋 Policy & Industry News

**[NEW] Trump budget proposal would slash CISA funding by $700M amid heightened nation-state threats**
The FY2027 budget proposal includes a $707 million cut to CISA, reducing the agency's budget from approximately $3 billion to just over $2 billion in discretionary funding. The proposal recycles language from last year's budget, referencing the elimination of programs that have already been shuttered. This cut comes as Chinese and Russian intelligence services are actively breaching U.S. government systems, including the FBI surveillance compromise disclosed today. [[CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/trump-budget-proposal-would-cut-hundreds-of-millions-more-from-cisa/)]

**[NEW] Anthropic terminates flat-rate Claude API access for third-party AI agent frameworks**
Anthropic has shut down OAuth-based subscription access for third-party tools like OpenClaw, forcing developers to transition to metered API billing at $0.50-$2.00 per agentic interaction. The change closes a loophole where power users piped premium Claude models into autonomous agents at flat monthly rates, creating what Anthropic described as "outsized strain" on infrastructure. While framed as a resource management decision, this effectively prices out solo developers and researchers building defensive security tools on Claude, concentrating advanced AI agent capabilities among well-funded organizations. [[CyberPress](https://cyberpress.org/anthropic-officially-terminates-claude-subscriptions/); [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/anthropic-ends-claude-subscription-access-for-openclaw/)]

**[NEW] Hims & Hers telehealth breach exposes customer support tickets via compromised Okta SSO**
Telehealth giant Hims & Hers Health confirmed that threat actors accessed millions of customer support tickets between February 4-7 through their Zendesk instance, after compromising an Okta SSO account. The ShinyHunters extortion gang—also linked to the European Commission breach—is behind this campaign, which has repeatedly targeted Zendesk deployments via stolen Okta credentials to access third-party SaaS platforms. Exposed data includes names, contact information, and support request details, though no medical records were compromised. This continues the pattern of ShinyHunters abusing Okta SSO to cascade into connected SaaS applications. [[BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hims-and-hers-warns-of-data-breach-after-zendesk-support-ticket-breach/)]
